How American Airlines Handles Name Changes

American Airlines distinguishes between minor corrections and complete name changes.

Minor Corrections

Minor corrections include simple spelling mistakes or typos that do not fundamentally alter the passenger’s identity. For example:

  • “Jhon” → “John”
  • “Smtih” → “Smith”

These changes are usually accepted with minimal fees, and sometimes even free if corrected immediately after booking.

Major Name Changes

Major changes involve legal name changes or completely different names. Examples include:

  • Name change due to marriage or divorce
  • Changing from “Mary Jane Smith” to “Mary Johnson”

Major changes may require additional documentation, such as a marriage certificate, court order, or government-issued ID. Fees may apply depending on the ticket type and airline policies.

American Airlines Name Change Fees

Fees for name changes vary depending on ticket type, route, and timing. While policies can change, here’s a general idea:

  • Domestic flights: Minor corrections often free; major changes may cost $150–$200.
  • International flights: Name corrections may cost $150–$300 or more.
  • Award tickets: Name changes are sometimes restricted; contact customer service for assistance.

Always check your ticket rules carefully. Certain discounted or promotional fares may have stricter rules and higher fees.
